Tiling Effect

You sit still for a moment before your actions. Then you are upon the rune tile, pressing down on that which spells strength first. With a twinge, your realize, it was attached to the floor and it snaps free and into your hand.

You lay the same force into a swift punch on against the precision tile and it breaks similarly.

Then you sit, meditating. The forest slumbers in your ears, a noiseless sound, full of life. You enter your wood, commune, seeking silence, answers.

A chirp awakens you. The door in front of you is open. A thin-legged bird with a tiny hat on hops idly at the doorway.

You sense a grace and a flexibility sustaining the limbs of your body.

[You are granted a temporary +1 boost to your Dexterity by your meditation]

Beyond the bird is a white-tiled hallway. Box-shaped elements protrude from the walls and are covered in tile too, making for a dizzying tunnel that stretches to a halt at the end of the corridor.

The Liar

You finishing the canteen in a few large gulps, take a bite of the rice and beans.

My apologies for any misgivings I have, but who are you and where am I? My name is Vidius Aindur, a member of our Lady of the Wood's Elidur. I am surprised you did not recognize the etchings yourselves.

You take another bite and reach for the bow in the elf's hands to stroke the etchings.

This bow is one of many allotted to us in the armory; these markings simply indicate when it was created. We use them as identifiers to catalog them, you lie, hiding the truth of the Ashwood of the Elidur.

[You are attempting to persuade the elves that your statement is the truth. You roll a 14 on a 20-sided die + 0 for your Charisma modifier]

Looking back at the elder elf, you wipe a grain of rice from your lip.

Apologies to disappoint, if you thought this bow retained any special abilities. To the best of my abilities, it was created..., you glance carefully at the markings, ..about 60 years ago, and matches a set of 6 others created by the craftsman that day.

The elf glances back at the bearded man and stands, taking the bow with him. They exchange mutters and the elf departs with his brethren.

I was with a close companion, another elf, have you any idea where he may be?

You direct the question at the departing elves, as the man closes the door behind them. He rubs at his wrists and says a few unhearable words. A stool, disassembled, flies in pieces through the slats of the windows and reassembles itself in front of him.

He sits on it and is whisked in front of you, the feet of the stool hovering inches above the floor.

False Elidur, he says, it seems time for a test.

His eyes burn down into yours, a darkness spreading from the pupils outward across the white and with a slight chant, he tosses a small powder across your face. The world blackens, flashes, and you are alone in the room.

On the ground at your knees are four ceramic tiles, each inscribed with an elvish rune, runes you recognize immediately.

"Strength"

"Precision"

"Silence"

"Justice"

Familiar Ashwood

You drink large but steady gulps of the canteen, never taking your eyes off of the human.

They watch you drink. You take a breath.

So, who are all of yall? Thank you for whatever you did to retrieve me from the horde that was descending on me. What happened?

The silverbeard rubs at his cheek and looks about. Our memory is shrouded, as is yours. He and I came with you, as it turns. He indicates the younger elf.

How long have I been out for?

A few days. Hence your thirst. He points at the canteen.

I am quite hungry yes, what do you have to offer? I could eat.

The elf nods, looks back at the bearded man who knocks on the door three times.

That is a nice bow you have there, I lost mine in the midst of that cacophony of Orcs.

Both elves absently play with the strings of their bows. The elder turns and grabs the bow he'd set beside the door. The door opens slightly, allowing someone to hand in a plate of pig, wild-rice and beans. He brings both bow and plate over and sets the plate in front of you.

Sipping on the water more, you ask, Where am I?

A question I will have a hard time answering, he says, crouching before you. High in the trees. But.. he holds the bow in front of your face. Looking at the etchings you'd watched engraved into the wood when the bow was being made, you immediately recognize your own bow, the familiar ashwood.. they want to know what these etchings mean.

Hello, Vidius

A pair of elves walk through the door, trailing each other as a breeze.

Both are armed, though the elder has two bows, one which he sets beside the door before turning to you.

You are feeling well, I hope? he says, seeming sure of the answer.

The younger elf walks a canteen over to you.

Water he says.

He returns to crouch at the inset of the door.

A young human man with wispy beard and crooked eyes steps in the door and stands at its entrance. He holds a slim stick. The crouching elf acknowledges him and then looks back at you.

Would you care for something to eat? the silverbeard asks. It would do you good.
